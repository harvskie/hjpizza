Pizza has a long history.
Flatbreads with toppings were consumed
by the ancient Egyptians, Romans and Greeks.
(The latter ate a version with herbs and oil,
similar to today's focaccia.)
But the modern birthplace of pizza is
southwestern Italy's Campania region,
home to the city of Naples.
